The Th-U evolution diagram is a scatter plot that sets out the 234U/238U-activity ratios against the 230Th/238U-activity ratios as error ellipses (drawn at 100[1-α]% confidence level), and displays the initial 234U/238U-activity ratios and ages as a set of intersecting lines. The Th-U evolution diagram has a similar and purpose and appearance as the U-Pb concordia diagram, which also displays compositions and dates simultaneously. An alternative way of doing so is to plot the 234U/238U-ratios against the 230Th/234U-238U-ages. In both types of evolution diagrams, IsoplotR provides the option to project the raw measurements along the best fitting isochron line and thereby remove the detrital 230Th-component. This procedure allows a visual assessment of the degree of homogeneity within a dataset, as is quantified by the MSWD.

Legend:

  1. age = t ± x | y | z

    where:

    t: the isochron age.
    x: the estimated uncertainty (σ) of t.
    y: the studentised 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD.
  2. (234U/238U)0 (230Th/232Th)0x intercept = i ± x | y | z

    where:

    i: the initial (234U/238U) excess (230Th/232Th) -ratio obtained by linear regression.
    x: the estimated uncertainty (σ) of i.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for i using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for i with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD. (only reported if MSWD > 1)
  3. MSWD = x, p(χ2) = y

    where:

    x: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) for the linear fit. MSWD values >> 1 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
    y: the Chi-squared p-value for the linear fit. p-values << 0.05 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
  4. dispersion = ω + u /- l

    where:

    ω: the excess ('geological') dispersion of the y-intercept, i.e. the standard deviation of the assumedly Gaussian distribution that describes the true non-radiogenic component.
    u: width of the upper half of a (1-α)% confidence interval for ω.
    l: width of the lower half of a (1-α)% confidence interval for ω.
