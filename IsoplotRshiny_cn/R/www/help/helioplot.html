The space of all possible U-Th-He compositions fits within the constraints of a ternary diagram or 'helioplot' (Vermeesch, 2008, 2010). If Sm is included as well, then this expands to a three-dimensional tetrahaedral space (Vermeesch, 2008). Conventional statistical analysis of data that fit within these constrained spaces is fraught with theoretical and practical problems that can be solved by applying a simple logratio transformation (Aitchison, 1982, 1986)

u ≡ ln[U/He],   v ≡ ln[Th/He]   (, w ≡ ln[Sm/He] ) (eq. 1)

and then performing the desired statistical analysis (averaging, uncertainty propagation, ...) on the transformed data (Vermeesch, 2008, 2010). Upon completion of these mathematical operations, the results can then be mapped back to U-Th-(Sm)-He space using an inverse logratio transformation:

[He] = 1/[eu+ev+(ew)+1];   [U] = eu/[eu+ev+(ew)+1];  
[Th] = ev/[eu+ev+(ew)+1];   ([Sm] = ew/[eu+ev+(ew)+1];  ) (eq. 2)

where [He] + [U] + [Th] (+ [Sm]) = 1. In the context of U-Th-(Sm)-He dating, the central age is defined as the age that corresponds to the arithmetic mean composition in logratio space, which is equivalent to the geometric mean in compositional dataspace (Vermeesch, 2008). The helioplot function visualises U-Th-(Sm)-He data on either a ternary diagram or a bivariate ln[Th/U] vs. ln[U/He] contour plot. These diagrams provide a convenient way to simultaneously display the isotopic composition of samples as well as their chronological meaning. In this respect, they fulfil the same purpose as the U-Pb concordia diagram and the U-series evolution plot.

Legend:

  1. central age = t ± x | y | z

    where:

    t: the central age, which is defined as the U-Th-He age corresponding to the weighted mean composition in log(U/He)-log(Th/He) space.
    x: the estimated standard deviation of t, estimated by the method of maximum likelihood.
    y: the studentised 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD.
  2. MSWD = x, p(χ2) = y

    where:

    x: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) of compositional equivalence for the U-Th-He measurements. MSWD values >> 1 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
    y: the Chi-squared p-value of equivalence for the geometric mean composition. p-values << 0.05 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
  3. dispersion = ω + u /- l %

    where:

    ω: the estimated standard deviation of the 'geological scatter' of the U/He, Th/He (and Sm/He) ratios required to explain the overdispersion of the U-Th-(Sm)-He compositions with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
    u: width of the upper half of a (1-α)% confidence interval for ω.
    l: width of the lower half of the (1-α)% confidence interval for ω.
